Zipfs Law in Classic Literature

Inspired by a video posted by the Youtube channel "Vsauce", I decided to analyze the occurrence of Zipf's law in the ten most popular English books available on Project Gutenberg. The law proposes that the frequency of a given word is proportional to the inverse of its ranking in a body of text. This law is used to roughly describe the distribution of words in human languages and has been applied in psychology, sociology, and economics.

To visualize Zipf's law, I first downloaded the 10 most popular English titles on the Project Gutenberg website as text files and removed irrelevant information (disclaimer, page numbers, etc.). I then wrote a program in python that opens all of the text files in its directory, stores the frequency of the words in a dictionary, and then produces the total word count, the total unique words, the word counts of the 10 most common words, and a graph showcasing the relationship between the ranking of a word and its frequency (created using the matplotlib library). The corpus of texts I used had over 1.2 million words and over 37 thousand unique words.

In addition, I wrote a report using LaTeX that explained Zipf's Law, my proccess, and the final results of my analysis.
