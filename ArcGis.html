On Demand Transit Analysis using ArcGIS Online

In April 2021, I represented the Dufferin Peel Catholic District School Board at the 2021 Virtual Skills Ontario Competition, where I placed second. For the event, I had to create a story map proposing an on-demand transit route for the city of Burlington (the prompt can be seen here). Optimizing tranist routes is an increasingly important matter, as it addresses the needs of our growing population while reducing emissions.

During this event, I applied my skills in ArcGIS, data analysis and data visualization to propose my solution to a panel of industry experts. In my final deliverable, I presented my workflow and conclusions alongside maps highlighting features such as

  • The proposed route with pick-up hubs
  • Areas where the senior population is over 25%
  • Areas where there is low ridership and a senior population over 25%
  • Population Density
  • Bus stops with low ridership and major roads.

Halfway through the competition, we were given time to show our work to the judges and receive feedback. I was able to implement their suggestions into my final deliverable, improving the quality of my proposal.

After the competition, my supervising teacher and I were interviewed by Guan Yue on the Esri Canada podcast "Geographical Thinking", where I discussed my experience.
