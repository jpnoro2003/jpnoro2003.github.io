NBA Salary Prediction Model

A screenshot of the interface, deployed on Streamlit, that allows users to see the predicted salary in the 2021-22 NBA season based on a player's season. Players can be searched through scraping their statistics using BeautifulSoup.

You can view the Model here.

Throughout the history of the NBA, player salaries have increased due to inflation and the increased popularity of the league. Additionally, player's salaries are based on a multitude of factors, including the year that they signed their contract, their team, and their statistics. As a result, two players who play similarly and have similar statistics might have vastly different contracts.

I developed a Random Forest Regression model that predicts a player's salary in the 2021-22 season based on their basic and advanced statistics (sourced from Basketball Reference), yielding a r-squared value of 0.836. I used Pandas and Seaborn to clean, join and visualize the data, allowing me to determine trends in the data. I then tested 5 different models from Scikit-Learn, fine-tuning their hyperparameters, which increased the r-squared value of my model by 10%.

The model was then deployed on Streamlit, allowing users to try the model themselves without having to install python on their machines. The website allows users to search for a player and select a specific year of their career, achieved by scraping player statistics from Basketball Reference using BeautifulSoup.
